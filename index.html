ANALYSIS OF HEAVY METALS IN BEVERAGES BY ICP-OES


Introduction

Nonalcoholic beverages are important food sources for adults and children all over the world. Many harmful pollutants in elemental form can be detected using Inductively Coupled Plasma-Optical Emission Spectrometry (ICP-OES).




Samples

Packaged beverage samples were collected from vendors, filtered and diluted 40:1 with Type 1 deionized water.

Standard Solutions

Standard solutions of concentrations (0.01mg/L, 0.05mg/L) for trace elements and for major elements (5mg/L, 20mg/L) were prepared by adding calculated volumes of the stock standard to 10ml of reagent water.

Conclusions

The detection and quantification of trace amounts of heavy metals like Al, Ni, Cd, V and Co was accomplished using the Agilent 720 ICP-OES.
